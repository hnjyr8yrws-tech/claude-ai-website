# v2.2 — Methodology Impact Report (Calibration Batch 1)

**Validation analysis of the five scored tools. No new tools scored.** What the v2.2
methodology actually *does* to scores, why, and the implications before any live rollout.
Source: `docs/v2.2-calibration-batch-1.md` + the standard in `~/Documents/methodology`.

## The five results vs the live baseline

| Tool | Basis | v2.2 | Verdict (v2.2) | Live baseline | Δ | Verdict shift |
|---|---|:-:|---|:-:|:-:|---|
| MagicSchool.ai | Hands-On (anchor) | 9.1 | Promptly Recommended | 9.2 | −0.1 | none |
| Quizizz / Wayground | Desk | 7.6 | Worth Considering | 8.5 | −0.9 | Strong Rec → Worth Considering |
| ClassDojo | Desk | 7.9 | Worth Considering | 9.4 | −1.5 | Promptly Rec → Worth Considering |
| Google Classroom | Desk | 8.0 | Strong Recommendation | 9.7 | −1.7 | Promptly Rec → Strong Rec |
| CPOMS | Desk | 7.5 | Worth Considering | 9.3 | −1.8 | Promptly Rec → Worth Considering |

**Every score fell. The four desk-reviewed tools fell 0.9–1.8; the one hands-on tool barely
moved (−0.1).** That single contrast is the headline finding.

## What's driving the drop (in order of impact)
1. **The Desk-Review ceiling (8.5 on Safeguarding & Privacy) is the dominant driver.** Those
   two pillars carry **60%** of the composite. Capping both at 8.5 mathematically caps a
   desk-only composite well below the top band. The hands-on anchor (MagicSchool, S 9.6 / P 8.5)
   keeps its top-band score; the desk tools cannot. **The −0.1 vs −0.9-to-−1.8 gap is almost
   entirely this.**
2. **The legal-barrier weighting (S 35% + P 25%).** v2.2 puts 60% of the score on the two
   pillars where desk evidence is weakest and conservative scoring is most warranted. Quality
   pillars (Age/Access/Transp = 40%) can't pull a composite up much.
3. **Conservative desk scoring.** Vendor self-assertions, gated DPAs and request-only VPATs
   mean a careful reviewer sits at 7.5–8.0, not 9, on the legal-barrier pillars.
4. **The graduated cap — did NOT bind in this batch.** All five weighted composites sat below
   `min(S,P)+1.0`. The cap is a latent guard here, not an active driver. It will bite only when
   one legal-barrier pillar is materially weaker than the average (see the migration's
   illustrative cases).
5. **Avoid trigger — not fired.** All S and P ≥ 4.5.

## Behavioural observations
- **Hands-on is the lever.** The methodology is *designed* so that documentary review caps you
  ~1.5 points below what hands-on/classroom evidence can earn. MagicSchool proves it: same
  rules, hands-on basis, 9.1. **Implication:** to keep flagship tools in the top band, we must
  invest in hands-on/classroom testing — desk review alone will systematically read as
  "Worth Considering."
- **Compression at the top.** The four desk tools cluster tightly (7.5–8.0). Under v2.2,
  desk-reviewed tools will mostly land 7–8 regardless of real quality differences — the ceiling
  flattens distinction. Worth checking this is the intended distribution.
- **Band-edge fragility.** Google Classroom (7.95) and ClassDojo (7.85) sit within 0.2 of a
  band boundary → both require a mandatory second-reader (§05A), and a ±0.5 pillar wobble flips
  their public verdict. Two of five being band-edge suggests the bands + desk ceiling interact
  to pile tools up near 7.9/8.0.
- **CPOMS caveat.** Its 7.5 partly rests on a *hollow* Safeguarding 8.0 (staff-only, no pupil
  exposure) — see the staff-system options paper. Treat CPOMS's number as provisional pending
  that ruling; it is not a clean data point.

## Cross-tool sanity check
Relative order under v2.2 (excluding the hands-on anchor): Google Classroom 8.0 > ClassDojo 7.9
> Quizizz 7.6 > CPOMS 7.5. Defensible on the evidence (Google's education-privacy governance +
independent WCAG edges it; CPOMS dragged by widget-only accessibility 5.5). No obvious
mis-ranking — but the spread (0.5) is narrow, which limits how much signal the number carries.

## Implications for a live rollout (the real risk)
- **Applying v2.2 to all ~242 live tools would drop most scores ~1–2 points and re-band a large
  share** (Promptly Recommended → Strong/Worth Considering). That is a significant public-facing
  change: vendor reactions, screenshots of "downgrades", and the need for a change-stamp on
  every score that moves ≥0.3 (§05A) — which will be almost all of them.
- **The current live scores are synthetic placeholders** (`derivePillars`), so the "delta" is
  partly an artefact of comparing real-ish v2.2 desk scores against fabricated baselines. The
  *true* comparison only exists once tools are genuinely re-reviewed.
- **The migration note stands:** do not push v2.2 to live scores until the calibration
  tolerances are confirmed (see the calibration plan).

## Recommendations
1. **Do not wire v2.2 into live scores/UI yet.** Keep in `docs/`.
2. **Decide the desk-ceiling stance consciously.** It is working exactly as written, but it
   means desk-only = "Worth Considering" almost always. Confirm that's intended, or revisit the
   ceiling / weighting before scaling.
3. **Prioritise hands-on testing for the flagship/most-visible tools** (the Homepage set) — it's
   the only way they reach Promptly Recommended under v2.2.
4. **Resolve CPOMS's hollow Safeguarding** via the staff-system ruling before trusting its score.
5. **Expect a migration/comms plan** for the inevitable mass re-banding before any go-live.
