## 1. Woebot — Avoid STANDS (verified: retired)
- **Verified disqualifier:** product **retired 30 June 2025**; account data anonymised 31 Jul 2025
  (woebothealth.com/faq). A retired product cannot be deployed.
- Verdict: **Avoid (product retired/unavailable)**. Evidence Confidence **High** (retirement verified).
- *Note:* the low pillar scores were partly absence-driven, but the retirement is dispositive — Avoid
  is correct on a verified ground.

## 2. Calm for Schools — Avoid STANDS, RE-GROUNDED (verified: privacy/terms)
- **Original ground was wrong:** Avoid (safeguarding 3.5) rested on *absence* of KCSIE/child-safety
  docs for what is a one-way, no-contact meditation library (low contact risk) — that absence is a
  confidence issue, not a verified harm.
- **Valid verified ground instead:** the **consumer privacy policy permits selling/sharing personal
  data with advertising partners for targeted ads** — a genuine, verified concern in a children-facing
  context — and the **16+ terms conflict with promoting under-16 student use**. These meet
  *"terms clearly unsuitable for school use."*
- Verdict: **Avoid (privacy/terms — verified).** Evidence Confidence **Medium-Low** (key pages 403'd;
  ad-data-sale verified via search extracts — confirm by direct fetch). **Missing:** school DPA,
  Children's-Code stance, direct policy fetch.

## 3. Headspace for Education — RE-GRADE to Proceed Carefully
- **Safeguarding Avoid was absence-driven:** the S=4.0 came from *no school safeguarding workflow / no
  DSL escalation / US-centric crisis routing* — i.e. not designed for school-managed pupil safeguarding,
  plus missing UK docs. It is a low-contact meditation library (the Ebb AI is 18+, so pupils don't get
  it). No **verified harm pathway** → does not meet the Avoid bar.
- **Genuine verified concern (keep, but not a disqualifier):** Headspace states it is **data controller**
  for most personal data and stores **US-only** — a real procurement/privacy issue a DPO must resolve.
  This legitimately tempers the Privacy pillar but is not, by itself, Avoid.
- Verdict: **Proceed Carefully.** Evidence Confidence **Low.** **Missing:** school DPA, processor terms,
  Children's-Code/KCSIE framing, VPAT detail. **Action:** DPO to assess controller posture + US transfer
  before any pupil use.

  verified harm.
- **Strong verified posture:** EU/Ireland hosting, ISO 27001 + Cyber Essentials, named sub-processors,

- Verdict (re-framed as a **staff wellbeing** tool): **Worth Considering** (Age/pupil-safeguarding N/A;
