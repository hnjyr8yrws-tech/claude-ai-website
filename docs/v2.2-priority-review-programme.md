**Planning only. No scoring. No evidence gathering.** Objective: the fastest route to *meaningful*
v2.2 coverage — i.e. covering the tools that are most seen and most consequential first, not all
242 at once. Reviewer of record: **CR**. Source: `~/Documents/methodology`.

## Effort model (stated assumptions)
- **Desk review** (gather cited evidence per pillar → score 5 pillars → rationale → record):
  **~1.5 h/tool**; a reviewer sustains **~5 desk reviews/day**.
- **Second-reader** (mandatory on Avoid + band-edge; ~25–30% of tools): **+0.5 h** each.
- **Hands-on test** (the only route past the 8.5 desk ceiling; recommended for high-visibility
  tools): **+2–3 h/tool**.
- Estimates are *reviewer-days for one reviewer*; parallelise across reviewers to compress.
  Blocked pages / vendor DPA-VPAT requests add lead time (not desk hours) — see the evidence-gap
  closure plan.

## Recommended review order (fastest route to meaningful coverage)
1. **Finish Tier 1 (now).** It is ~70% done and it's the set users actually see — completing it
   gives **meaningful public-facing coverage in ~1 week**. Run the **calibration tolerance
   validation** in parallel (it gates publishing anyway).
2. **Tier 2, in this sub-order:**
   1. **Safeguarding (16)** — highest child-risk; most likely to surface the first real **Avoids**
      and **cap-binding** cases (also fills the low-band calibration anchors).
   2. **SEND & Inclusion (34)** — brand-critical, accessibility-heavy; large block, high value.
   3. **Assessment & Feedback (18)** — heavily used, pupil-facing.
   4. **Teacher Productivity (13)** — includes the general LLMs already drafted in Tier 1; finish
      the rest.
3. **Tier 3 (last)** — batch by category, lowest traffic first-out, highest-traffic-within-tail
   first-in.

## Meaningful-coverage milestones
- **M1 (≈1 week):** Tier 1 complete + tolerances validated → every *homepage/featured* tool
  carries a defensible v2.2 score. **This is the recommended point to consider a limited go-live
  discussion** (public-facing tools only).
- **M2 (≈4–5 weeks):** Tier 1 + Safeguarding + SEND → the highest-stakes decision tools covered.
- **M3 (≈10–12 weeks / faster parallelised):** full 242.

## Why this is the fastest route
Coverage that *matters* ≠ all 242. ~**44%** of the catalogue (Tiers 1+2 = 106 tools) covers
everything a school sees on the homepage plus every high-stakes category — and Tier 1 alone is
nearly finished. Front-loading those reaches defensible, publishable coverage of the tools that
carry reputational and safeguarding weight **weeks before** the long tail is touched.
