**Method (per §05A):** pillars scored 0–10 in 0.5 steps · weighted sum at full precision
(Safeguarding 35% · Data Privacy 25% · Age 15% · Accessibility 15% · Transparency 10%) ·
graduated cap `composite ≤ min(Safeguarding, Privacy) + 1.0` · round half-up to 1 dp ·
verdict band assigned. **Desk Review ceiling (max 8.5 on Safeguarding & Privacy)** applied to the
four desk-reviewed tools. **MagicSchool.ai is the hands-on anchor** (sub-scores from Brand Bible
v1.1), so the desk ceiling does not apply to it — it validates the calculation pipeline.

## Calibration observations
1. **Desk basis dominates the outcome.** The four desk tools all land **Worth Considering / Strong Recommendation**, not "Recommended", because the 8.5 ceiling caps their two heaviest pillars. Reaching 9.0+ requires hands-on/classroom evidence — by design.
2. **Two band-edge composites (Google Classroom 7.95, ClassDojo 7.85)** fall within 0.2 of a boundary → **mandatory second-reader** per §05A. Small pillar moves flip their verdict band.
3. **The cap never bound** in this batch — useful as a baseline; the migration's illustrative cases show it biting only when a legal-barrier pillar is materially weaker than the average.
4. **CPOMS exposes a methodology gap:** the Safeguarding pillar definition (child-harm exposure) doesn't fit a staff-only safeguarding MIS. Needs a written ruling before scoring similar admin/MIS tools (Satchel One next).
5. **Accessibility is the most volatile pillar** — independently-verified ACRs (ClassDojo, Google Classroom) score well; request-only/widget-only ones (Quizizz, CPOMS) score low and uncertain. Verifying VPATs will move scores most.
6. **All deltas are negative** vs the live baselines (−0.1 to −1.8), confirming the migration's prediction that v2.2 pulls scores down. **Do not apply these to live scores** until calibration tolerances are confirmed.

## Flagged uncertainty (whole batch)
- **Reviewer:** AI desk-draft — **no named human reviewer, no blind scoring, no second reader** yet (all required by §05A/§05C before any score is defensible).
- **Evidence:** vendor self-assertion verified only as published text, not behaviourally tested; several DPAs/VPATs are request-only/NDA-gated; some pages were 403/JS-rendered (re-verify in a browser).
- **Tolerances:** the ±0.5 pillar / ±0.3 composite calibration tolerances are **provisional** — these drafts must be double-scored against them before anything is trusted.
